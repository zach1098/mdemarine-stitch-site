What this page should say

The work before the work starts

Most engine projects go sideways because nobody nails down the failure, the access plan, or the real install scope early enough. This page fixes that. It tells people what kind of support they can ask for and what information they need to bring.

Diagnostics

Problem calls

Use this for boats that already have a problem and need a clear read on what is actually failing.

  • No-start, low power, smoke, or overheat complaints
  • Oil, coolant, or fuel leak tracing
  • Noise, vibration, or alignment concerns
Repowers

Change-out planning

For projects where the engine is being removed or replaced and the hard part is coordinating the full scope without missing hidden work.

  • Removal path and access review
  • Fitment questions and engine-room constraints
  • Mounting, disconnect, exhaust, cooling, and controls scope
Execution

Yard coordination

The site should make it obvious whether this is dockside troubleshooting or a yard job that needs planning, crane time, or fabrication support.

  • Haul-out and schedule-sensitive jobs
  • Crane and lift coordination
  • Scope cleanup before labor starts running
Good intake beats good marketing

What owners and captains should send first

  • Engine make, model, serial number, and hours if known
  • Current symptoms, recent alarms, and what changed before the issue started
  • Photos of the engine room, service access, and the affected parts
  • Whether the vessel is in the water, hauled out, or waiting on a yard slot
  • Whether the job is diagnosis only, repair, or full replacement planning

This keeps the page honest. It does not promise magic. It tells people what information leads to a useful first conversation.

Typical scope buckets

How the work usually breaks down

  • Quick dockside troubleshooting to confirm the likely failure
  • Mechanical repair plan for components that can be fixed in place
  • Repower planning when repair cost, reliability, or downtime makes replacement smarter
  • Cross-page handoff to fabrication when mounts, supports, or install modifications are needed
  • Cross-page handoff to parts sourcing when lead times or part ID are the real blocker
